What a Revenue Audit Actually Finds: 5 Patterns From Real Client Diagnostics

Every revenue audit feels unique to the company going through it. From the outside, running enough of them, the same five patterns show up again and again - different industries, different deal sizes, same root causes. If two or more of these land, you're not an edge case. You're the pattern.

1. The CRM is a graveyard of stale deals

Pipeline value looks healthy because nobody purges dead deals - closing a deal as "lost" feels like admitting failure, so it just sits there, open, inflating the number everyone's making decisions from. This is the single biggest reason forecasts miss.

2. Sales and marketing have never agreed on "qualified" out loud

Both teams assume they share a definition. They don't - and it's never been said in the same room, so the mismatch resurfaces every quarter as a blame cycle instead of getting fixed once.

3. The best rep's process was never extracted

One person is closing at two or three times the team average, and their actual method - the specific questions, the sequencing, the objection handling - exists nowhere except their own head. Every new hire re-learns it from scratch, slowly, if at all.

4. Everyone watches lagging indicators

Closed revenue is the metric on every leadership dashboard, and it's the least actionable one - it tells you what already happened. The leading indicators that would've predicted it eight weeks earlier usually aren't tracked anywhere.

5. Effort and opportunity size are uncorrelated

Without a real signal-scoring framework, the deals getting the most attention are whichever ones are loudest this week - not the ones actually worth the most, or closest to closing.

Why the same five, every time

None of these are unique to any one company. They're what happens by default when a revenue engine grows past what one founder's instincts can hold together, and nobody's built the system to replace it yet. That's not a criticism - it's just what happens, until someone deliberately fixes it.
