Why Your Best Sales Reps Can't Save a Broken Deliverability Setup

Here's a conversation I've had with more revenue leaders than I can count: pipeline is shrinking, close rates are dropping, and everyone's looking at the sales team. New talk tracks get written. Reps get coached. A new CRM stage gets added. Nothing moves.

Then someone checks inbox placement, and half of outbound - cold and warm follow-up - has been landing in spam or promotions for six weeks. The sales problem was never a sales problem.

Why revenue leaders miss this

Deliverability lives in a blind spot between sales, marketing, and IT - sales assumes email "just works" because it's not their system to own, marketing looks at campaign-level metrics that don't surface individual sender reputation decay, and IT isn't watching the sales team's actual send behavior. Nobody owns it, so nobody catches it until pipeline has already been bleeding for a quarter.

This is the piece of revenue leadership almost nobody with a traditional sales background actually understands - it sits below the CRM, below the playbook, in the infrastructure email rides on. I spent years in email deliverability before revenue strategy, and it's the first place I look when pipeline metrics don't add up.

Four checks to run this week

1. Check actual inbox placement, not just open rate

Open rate tells you almost nothing anymore - Apple Mail Privacy Protection and similar features inflate it artificially. Use Google Postmaster Tools (if your volume qualifies) or a placement-testing tool to see where mail is actually landing: primary inbox, promotions, or spam.

2. Confirm SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are aligned - not just present

Having these records isn't the same as having them correctly aligned with your actual sending domain and configured to quarantine or reject rather than none. Misalignment is one of the most common silent killers of sender reputation, and it can happen from a single unrelated marketing tool change.

3. Check whether sales is sending from your primary domain

If reps are sending high-volume outbound from the same domain your product and support run on, one bad sending pattern can drag down deliverability for transactional and warm-lead email too. A dedicated sending subdomain, properly warmed, isolates the risk.

4. Track reply rate trend, not just volume sent

Reply rate is a leading indicator of placement that most dashboards ignore. A steady decline in reply rate with stable send volume and stable list quality is one of the earliest signals that deliverability - not message quality - is degrading.

The revenue leadership takeaway

If your buyer signal scoring looks fine on paper but conversion keeps slipping anyway, don't start with the talk track. Start with whether your signal is even reaching the buyer's inbox. This is exactly the kind of cross-domain gap a real revenue audit is built to catch.
